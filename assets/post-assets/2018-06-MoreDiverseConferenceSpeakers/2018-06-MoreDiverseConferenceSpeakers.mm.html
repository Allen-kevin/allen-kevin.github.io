Goal: Increase underrepresented conference speakers

  • People who feel unsafe at conferences
    • Submit talks
      • Clear COC
      • Conf staff trained on COC
      • Clear COC Enforcement policy
      • Spaces w/o alcohol as highlights of the conf (not afterthought)
      • Visible / recognizable safety staff
      • Quick / easy conduct report system
      • Public conduct report system (to ensure org accountability)
      • Include safety information with COC (police, local authorities, etc.)
      • Anonymous "concern report" to surface potential issues with speakers or organizers
  • Women
    • Submit talks

      • Reach out and invite them directly

        Invite them for their expertise

        Reach out with suggested topics based on expertise

        Don't invite them to a WIT panel if you haven't invited them for another reason too

        Don't ask them to be a "woman speaker" or say your conference needs more diversity (that's not why you're inviting them, even if it's true)

  • POC
    • Submit talks
      • Invite them directly for their expertise
      • Reach out with suggested topics
      • Don't say you need more POC speakers (even if you do; that's why you're not inviting them)
  • Moms
    • Submit talks
      • Make a stocked mothers' room available & close to conf space
      • Provide free or subsidized child care services
      • Subsidize family / child travel costs
  • UR speakers with financial barriers
    • Submit talks

      • Cover travel costs

        Offer a stipend to those whose companies don't support traveling to speak

        Provide cost coverage for 1 additional person of speaker's choice -- so someone speaking also provides an opportunity to others

        Give free advertising to employers who cover UR speaker costs (shout-outs, tweets, etc.)

        Scholarship for UR attendees (more diverse attendees is more attractive to diverse speakers)

  • LGBTQIA+ speakers
    • Submit talks
      • Gender neutral bathrooms
      • Inclusive & welcoming language everywhere
      • Free-form / decline option for any gender demographics / language
  • Speakers w/disabilities
    • Submit talks
      • Draft an accessibility statement
      • Set expectations clearly on site (length of sessions, building accessibility)
      • Ensure venue has appropriate access & advertise it
  • UR speakers with time barriers
    • Submit talks
      • Publish conference info as early as possible
      • Ensure a long CFP time (to reach out, gain interest, and let people craft submissions)
  • Overrepresented Speakers
    • Amplify conferences looking for UR speakers
    • Tie your participation to others' inclusion
  • UR speakers w/imposter syndrome
    • Submit talks
      • Nomination forms to suggest a talk by a UR speaker
      • Make space for first-time speakers
      • Offer coaching sessions / mentorship office hours to help craft submissions
      • Make the CFP process easy to navigate. Clear language, link to help if you get stuck, etc.
      • Suggest topics (with input from UR communities)
      • List past selected topics
      • Showcase accepted proposals to show what you look for
      • Link to encouraging sites, e.g. WAAA
  • Conference organizers
    • More direct outreach
      • Organizer team members dedicated to outreach
      • Partner with local underrepresented groups
      • Ask prominent people in underrepresented communities to amplify
      • Diverse conference organizers
    • Understand underrepresented communities
      • Diverse organizer teams
      • Invite / pay consultants from underrepresented groups
    • Make diversity & representation a stated goal of the conference
      • Text on the home page
      • Diverse conference organizers
      • Text on CFP
      • Mention it along with public tweets, etc.
      • Space dedicated to all the things you're offering for UR speakers
      • Mention an anonymous selection process
    • Understand why people don't submit
      • Submission form -- if someone doesn't submit, ask them why
      • Diverse conference organizers
      • On the space dedicated to "diversity", ask "if this isn't enough, let us know and we'll fix it."
  • Advertisers
  • Selection committees
    • Choose more underrepresented speakers
      • "blind" submission process
      • decide in advance to do more outreach if speakers are too homogenous
      • Ensure the selection committee is diverse
